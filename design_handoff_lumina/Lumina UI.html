UI · LUMINA

A calmer, lighter editor.

Same bones as your current build — sidebar, toolbar, editor — just with everything that wasn't earning its place removed. All-sans (Inter Tight). Indigo used sparingly, and only where it helps you locate something. Below: dark mode, light mode, and the rationale for each move.

What changed, and why

In service of "lightweight and out of the way."
1

Toolbar: full action set, with a Format dropdown

Six clusters separated by hairline dividers: history · format · inline · lists · blocks · insert. H1/H2/H3 collapse into a single Format dropdown that also exposes Paragraph — so the editor can tell you what kind of block you're in, and switch it. Every icon has a tooltip on hover (with its keyboard shortcut), so nothing is mystery glyphs.

2

Sidebar lost the path noise

Just filename + relative time ("2 hours ago"). Full path appears on hover. The active file gets a 3px indigo bar and a soft tint — locatable at a glance, not loud.

3

Search promoted to the top of the sidebar

⌘K. Files-first; folder tree (when there are many) can come later. The folder context lives at the bottom — present, not prominent.

4

Editor canvas given real margins

640px max-width content column with 56px side padding. Reading length matches print. The page itself gets to feel like a page.

5

Title bar shows what's open + the app

The app icon, the filename, then "· Lumina" in muted color. No competing label up top — it doubles as identity.

6

Status bar is one line of useful info

Format · Encoding · Cursor position, with a "Saved" pill on the right. Calm, scannable, gone if you're not looking.

7

Light mode is warm, not stark

Page on #FAFAF7 with sidebar on #F4F2EE. Soft, paper-like. Easier on the eyes than pure white-on-white.

8

Inter Tight throughout

One typeface, three weights (400/500/700). Display feel for headings, clean body, monospaced for code/metadata. Removes a layer of decision-making and looks unified.

Where the indigo shows up

Used as a "you are here" signal — not decoration. If you removed it, the design would still work; it's just helpful.
